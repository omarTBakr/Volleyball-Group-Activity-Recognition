\begin{abstract}
   A condensed summary of the Volleyball Group Activity Recognition project: the data pipeline, the two completed baselines (B1: single-frame ResNet-50; B3: person-then-group crop classifier), and the two silent data bugs whose discovery and repair took Baseline 3 from 23\% to 61\% test accuracy. The dataset (Ibrahim et al., CVPR 2016) contains 55 videos / 4{,}830 clips with 8 scene-level group activities and 9 player-level actions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem \& Dataset}

    \taustart{V}olleyball Group Activity Recognition is a hierarchical classification problem: predict the scene-level group activity (8 classes: \texttt{l/r}-pass, -spike, -set, -winpoint) and the per-player action (9 classes: blocking, digging, falling, jumping, moving, setting, spiking, standing, waiting). The dataset comes from \href{https://www.cs.sfu.ca/~mori/research/papers/ibrahim-cvpr16.pdf}{``A Hierarchical Deep Temporal Model for Group Activity Recognition''} (Ibrahim et al., CVPR 2016; \href{https://github.com/mostafa-saad/deep-activity-rec}{official repo}, \href{https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ahmedmohamed365/volleyball}{Kaggle mirror}).

Key facts: \textbf{55 videos, 4{,}830 clips} (train/val/test = 2{,}152/1{,}341/1{,}337 clips over disjoint video splits of 24/15/16). Each clip has \textbf{41 frames}; the clip folder is named after its annotated \emph{middle frame}. Both label levels are heavily skewed: \texttt{standing} is $\approx$68\% of person actions, and \texttt{l/r\_winpoint} are $\approx$2.5$\times$ rarer than the other group classes. The group classes come in \textbf{left/right mirror pairs}, so horizontal geometry is part of the label --- no horizontal flips, no crops that discard court width.

\section{Data Pipeline}

Three raw annotation sources (action detections, person detections, player tracking) are unified by a two-stage parser (\texttt{src/json\_parser.py}) into one master JSON keyed \texttt{video\_id/clip\_id}, then enriched with the group-activity label per clip and cached as a pickle ($\sim$247\,MB) for instant metadata loading. Frames are served either from a memory-mapped LMDB (local) or straight from disk (Kaggle); both backends expose the identical \texttt{VolleyballDataset}, which covers every baseline through flags: full frames or per-player crops, 1 or 9 frames per clip. A custom collate pads the variable player count per clip and returns a validity mask so padded slots never reach the loss or the pooling.

\section{Milestone: Two Silent Data Bugs}

Both baselines were initially stuck near chance level regardless of hyperparameters. Two input-corruption bugs --- neither of which ever raised an error --- were found and fixed in July 2026:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Label corruption (62\% of the dataset).} The scene-label merge matched clips against a \emph{flat} frame-name dictionary, but frame names are only unique within a video; 3{,}001 of 4{,}830 clips received another clip's label. Fixed by keying the lookup per video and matching each clip's own middle frame; all 4{,}830 labels now verify against the per-video ground truth.
    \item \textbf{Transforms destroyed the input.} Person crops (median $56 \times 127$\,px) went through a short-side resize + $224$ center crop that kept only $\approx$40\% of the player's height --- classifying arm/leg actions from torsos. Full frames similarly lost over half the court width, deleting the left/right signal. Both pipelines now warp directly to $224 \times 224$ with mild photometric augmentation only.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Baseline 1: Single-Frame Classifier}

Fine-tunes a pretrained ResNet-50 on the clip's middle frame for the 8 group classes. Two-stage schedule: linear probe (head only, 10 epochs, lr $10^{-3}$), then partial fine-tune of \texttt{layer3/4} + head (up to 100 epochs, backbone lr $2.5 \times 10^{-4}$, head $3\times$, cosine annealing, batch 16, frozen BatchNorm held in eval).

After the label fix but still on the legacy transforms, B1 reached \textbf{62.98\% test accuracy / 0.641 macro F1} (up from 20.8\% / 0.196 under corrupted labels); Fig.~\ref{fig:b1-confusion} shows its confusion matrix. A retrain on the fully corrected pipeline (full-court warp, batch 16, rescaled lr) is in progress and will replace these numbers.

\section{Baseline 3: Person-Then-Group}

\textbf{Stage A} fine-tunes ResNet-50 on individual player crops for the 9 person actions with inverse-frequency class-weighted CE (without it the loss collapses onto \texttt{standing}). \textbf{Stage B} freezes that backbone, extracts a $(P, 2048)$ feature matrix per clip, concatenates masked max- and mean-pooling across players ($2 \times 2048$), and trains an MLP ($4096 \to 512 \to 8$, Dropout 0.4) with class-weighted CE for the group classes. SGD (momentum 0.9, Nesterov), 50 epochs and patience 10 per stage, batch 16 clips, \texttt{nn.DataParallel} on multi-GPU hosts.

\begin{table}[h]
    \centering
    \caption{Baseline 3 before/after the data-pipeline fixes.}
    \label{tab:b3-run-compare}
    \begin{tabular}{lcc}
        \toprule
        & \textbf{run 1 (pre-fix)} & \textbf{run 2 (fixed)} \\
        \midrule
        Stage A val acc / F1 & 59.7\% / 0.387 & 70.4\% / 0.512 \\
        Stage B val acc / F1 & 39.2\% / 0.370 & 60.0\% / 0.584 \\
        Test acc / F1        & 23.1\% / 0.227 & \textbf{60.7\% / 0.589} \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}

Stage A's gain isolates the transform fix (its labels were never corrupted); Stage B compounds the corrected labels with the stronger backbone. Fig.~\ref{fig:b3-confusion} shows the dominant remaining error mode: left/right mirror confusion within each activity pair, worst on the rare \texttt{r\_winpoint}. The other open issue is \textbf{overfitting} (train $\approx$95\% vs.\ val 58--70\%) --- a regularization problem for a follow-up run, not a data issue.

\section{Next Steps}

(i) Finish the B1 retrain on the corrected pipeline; (ii) attack B3's overfitting (stronger augmentation, higher dropout, earlier stopping); (iii) proceed to the temporal baselines B4--B8 --- noting that player tracking covers only $\approx$half of each clip's frames, so the detection-fallback path of the loader must be label-corrected before B5.
